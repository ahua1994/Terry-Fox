Terry Fox

Terry Fox was a Canadian hero who attempted to run across Canada, on one leg, to raise funds for cancer research.





  • July 28, 1958 -Birthday - Terrance Stanley Fox is born in Winnipeg, Manitoba.
  • January 01, 1966 - Family Leaves Winnipeg - The Fox family moves west and eventually settles in Port Coquitlam, a suburb east of Vancouver.
  • March 04, 1977 - Diagnosis - He is diagnosed with osteogenic sarcoma, a tumour which starts in the knee and spreads through the muscles and tendons.
  • March 09, 1977 - Amputation - When Fox is only 18, doctors amputate his right leg 15 cm above the knee. He soon begins physiotherapy and a 16-month chemotherapy program.
  • February 01, 1979 - Training - Wishing not to be conquered by his condition, he begins training for his Cancer Research funding marathon across Canada.
  • April 12, 1980 - Marathon of Hope - He begins his run from the Eastern Coast of Canada.
  • September 01, 1980 - Marathon Ends - After running 5373km in 143 days and raising $2 million dollars,
                                          Terry Fox stops his run due to chest pains. X-rays reveal the cancer has spread to his lungs.
  • February 01, 1981 - Donations continue and Terry reaches his goal of $1 per Canadian; $24.17 million dollars.
  • June 28, 1981 - Terry Contracts pneumonia and goes into a coma on the 27th of June. At 4am the following morning, he passes away one month before his 23rd birthday.
